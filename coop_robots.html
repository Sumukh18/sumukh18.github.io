Guide: Dr. Thiyagarajan R, IIT Tirupati

[Jan'23 - May'24]

The project is centered around the developing 3-Wheel Omnidirectional Mobile Robots equipped with Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) capabilities and Cooperative Navigation. It represents an exciting and ambitious venture within the field of robotics, with the primary goal of designing and constructing autonomous mobile robots that possess a high level of versatility and capability. These robots are intended to serve a multitude of practical applications, making them a significant advancement in the field of robotics.

At the core of our project are 3-wheel omnidirectional mobile robots, equipped with advanced sensory systems, including cameras and laser sensors, to enable seamless SLAM and autonomous navigation. These robots are designed to navigate complex environments with a high degree of precision and autonomy.

One of the key innovations in our project is the integration of a Cooperative Navigation system. For pairs of robots, we have implemented linear formations, allowing them to work together effectively in a coordinated manner. For groups of three robots, we've employed triangular arrangements, enhancing their collaborative capabilities. This cooperative approach opens up new possibilities for these robots, enabling them to tackle tasks that would be challenging for individual units.

The heart of our robots comprises a Raspberry Pi 4 as the microprocessor, providing the computational power needed for complex navigation and decision-making tasks. Additionally, a Raspberry Pi Pico serves as the microcontroller, facilitating low-level hardware control and communication. This combination of hardware components ensures that our robots are both intelligent and responsive.

In terms of functionality, these robots excel in several areas. They can autonomously navigate through dynamic and static obstacles, thanks to advanced SLAM techniques. The integration of micro-ROS with the Raspberry Pi Pico allows for seamless communication between the high-level Robot Operating System (ROS2) environment and the robot's hardware, enhancing adaptability and responsiveness.

Throughout the development process, we are paying meticulous attention to calibrate motors and fine-tune the PID control algorithms, ensuring the robots' stability and accuracy in their movements. Extensive testing and debugging will be carried out to guarantee the robustness of the system, making it adaptable to a wide range of environments and scenarios.

To ensure the modularity and ease of communication among different modules, we have implemented the Robot Operating System (ROS2) Humble framework. This choice allows us to reuse code, maintain a high level of modularity, and seamlessly integrate various functionalities, making our robots versatile and adaptable to diverse applications.
